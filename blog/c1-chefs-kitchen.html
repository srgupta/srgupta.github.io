Creating vs. Consuming:

Initial Thoughts on DBC's Learning Philosophy

December, 2014

"When we learn, we make ourselves vulnerable. When we engage in learning, we communicate that we want to grow, to become better, to improve ourselvesâ€¦" - George Siemens, learning theorist

As an undergraduate, I majored in Physics and Math. Whenever I told people that, they usually treated me as some really technical person. I never liked it, because what attracted me to those subjects most were in fact deeply human: imagination, creativity, inspiration, curiosity and wonder. I learned alot in college, but like many students I found that the reality of formal education often didn't match up to the deep sense of engagement true learning provides.

I was attracted to DBC because of its emphasis on learning pedagogy, but after watching Shereef talk about the experiment that is DBC, I'm kinda sold. I love how developing a kitchen mindset - the assumption that our experience of learning is entirely dependent not just on what DBC provides, but what we bring to table too - is built in to the program. I love how producing - rather than consuming - our learning is expected, not some afterthought. And I love how when everyone is willing to co-create - to give and to receive - from day one offers the potential to truly change the game when it comes to how much we can transform ourselves. In sum, I love how being human - curious, creative, vulnerable - will be inherent to how we learn to code.

I suppose there are risks. Namely, will I be able to express my fears and vulnerabilities, when I've spent a lifetime avoiding them???
